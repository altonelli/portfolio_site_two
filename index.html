discoverfy.

discover new music based on your spotify music tastes.

This is my first fully developed iOS app. It connects to a users Spotify account to retreive a users saved songs and playlists and store them on the phone using CoreData. A user is then presented 30 second previews of music based on their most listened to songs, extracting any songs that were in a playlist. If a user likes a song they can swipe right and save the to a "Discoverfy" playlist in Spotify. If they do not like the preview, a user can swipe left and the song will be saved in a database and not be shown again.

Along side the iPhone app, I created a simple Node.js server and MongoDB database to store songs the user has listened to and also handle the OAuth verification.

Networking. CoreData. OAuth. GCD. Multithreading.

ava.

a voice recognition personal assistant

This was my final project at General Assembly. I wanted to create an app that was not a standard "CRUD" app. I am entrigued by new technologies. Encouraged by Siri and Alexa, I decided to create my own voice recogonition app. I used Wit.ai API to handle the voice recognition, several external API's to retrieve data, and Google Synth to speak back to the user. Due to the technologies used, you must be using Chrome or Opera to use the app.

You can ask Ava for the weather, to search Google for something, directions, and near by points of interest, such as coffee shops and restaurants. Don't forget to say hi to her!

Voice Recognition. Angular. External APIs. Asynchonous Loading.

chorus.

an app to keep track of your apartment's chores.

This was a group project at General Assembly. It is a chore tracking app for roommates and shared living spaces. I did the database architecture and set up the initial API routes. A key feature that I built out was roommate requests to grant approval into the unit. Beyond that I acted as project manager, keeping our team on track and helping in paired programming.

I have several friends with visual impairments. After General Assembly they encouraged me to learn web accessibility. I adjusted the front-end to conform to WCAG standards and make the app usable for screen readers and tab inputs.

Ruby on Rails. PostgreSQL. Accessibility. ARIA. Auth.
